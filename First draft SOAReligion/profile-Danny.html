MEET DANNY YAHINI

Danny Yahini, 66, now works as a custom builder in Athens, Ohio. However, Yahini grew up far from Athens, in Israel. He was drawn to Athens in 1980 by the progressive community and the opportunity to build his own house in the woods.

Yahini grew up Jewish but never felt spiritually drawn to the religion. As someone who regularly practices yoga and meditation, Yahini found inspiration in the practice of Zen. This eventually led him to explore the teachings of Buddhism.

ABOUT HIS PRACTICE

Traditionally, Tibetan Buddhists venerate many different deities. Like many modern Tibetan Buddhists, however, Yahini does not necessarily believe in these deities, but he does believe in the teachings of Tibetan Buddhism. Yahini uses these teachings as a path to spiritual growth.

Tibetan Buddhism focuses a lot on mindfulness. It is important for Buddhists to be aware of their emotions and critical of their worldly desires. According to Buddhist teachings, people can find peace by pursuing enlightenment rather than worldly desires like money.

Yahini described his findings, “you can't work on your mind without realizing at some point there's more to it than just this material plane.”

WHERE HE PRACTICES: ATHENS KARMA THEGSUM CHÖLING (KTC)

Yahini describes the Athens KTC as a very diverse group of open-minded people. While some members are practicing Tibetan Buddhists, other members are more interested in non-religious spiritual growth. Almost all members hail from outside of Athens, and even a few from outside of the United States. Some members are shy, while others are more outgoing. Yahini says the group welcomes everyone and all ideas, which makes the Sangha stronger as a whole.

Athens KTC, located inside Factory Street Dance Studio. Still from video by Max Catalano.



MEDITATING TOGETHER, SEPARATELY

Tibetan Buddhism is not widely practiced in the United States. Almost all Sanghas—Buddhist communities that assemble together—are found in big cities, though some large cities do not have a Sangha in the area at all. However, fifteen years ago, Yahini helped found Athens Karma Thegsum Choling (Athens KTC). Athens KTC is an informal group of people who come together to practice and discuss Tibetan Buddhist teachings. The Athens KTC meets every Sunday at the Factory Street Dance Studio for meditation and study. Yahini believes the Athens Sangha thrives because of its location in a small, open-minded community.
